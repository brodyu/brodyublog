As the United States and most of Western Europe battle the COVID-19 crisis, normal life for most people has come to a halt. Many states in America are going into lockdown and forcing millions of Americans to shelter in place. This black swan event has prompted many investors to speculate on almost all facets of industries affected by the crisis. Today, I will discuss the industries that are often overlooked from most investors but still have room for speculation.

Google Trends

To analize trends, we will use Google Trends, a free platform developed by Google to analyze the popularity of search queries and topics utilizing Google Search. Google Trends will allow us to quickly see what is trending and where we want to possibly investment. The best part about Google Trends is that I can quickly add the scripts to any HTML page, like this one, for you to view.

Working From Home

The COVID-19 pandemic caused millions of workers across America to start working from home. While most will probably return back to work in a few months, we will definitely see a new generation of permenant remote workers. The pandemic has forced many businesses to adapt and now employers are starting to see the feasibility of remote work.

One of the most obvious winners from the work at home trend is computer hardware companies, like AAPL and MSFT. Google search queries for the keywords 'laptop' and 'macbook' have shot up dramatically. What is surprising is that current search traffic exceeds the amount seen on Christmas and Black Friday. This shows that remote workers and students are investing heavily in new laptops and computer gear to make the transition to working at home as seamless as possible.

While distributers like Best Buy and semiconductors might also benefit from the rise in computer hardware purchases, the clear winner remains the manufacturers themselves.

One interesting worldwide trend is that 'ipad' and 'macbook' google search queries are sustaining high numbers. Sustained search query numbers are rare and point to the possibility of bigger numbers than black friday and christmas season sales.

New Pet Adoptions

While many adjust to a new life at home, an interesting and overlooked trend is emerging. People are adopting pets more than ever before. Google Trends shows an expoential increase for the search term 'Humane soceity adopt', which shows people are all over America are interested in adopting a pet during their time in quarantine. Moreover, we can speculate that once a pet is adopted/fostered, they will remain in the household for years to come.

Chewy

One of the potential winners from the surge in pet adoptions is Chewy, an online retailer of pet food and pet-related products. In the midst of quaratine, Chewy's e-commerce platform is provided a huge opportunity for growth and a whole new potential customer base.

Google Trends search traffic for Chewy.com has seen record levels from previous declining 12-month searches.

Chewy's current price is up 15% for the month due to a rise in all e-commerce related platforms because of the COVID-19 crisis. However, Chewy stands out from the crowd by targeting the pet industry. I dont believe investors are valuing Chewy on the basis of pet adoptions, but only for its e-commerce platform, which makes this play overlooked. Chewy has a huge growth opportunity if they are able to capitalize and retain this new incoming customer base.
